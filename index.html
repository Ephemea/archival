About

Ephemea Archival is a privately owned archival effort. Ephemea Archival bases itself around collecting and preserving military documents & books, though also including civilian books. The long term mission is to preserve and hold military history for many years, ensuring that research and interest in such provided will be avaliable for years to come.

Glossary

Definitions and meanings behind the prefixes used across the archive catalog:

UCS
Uniform Code System (UCS) - A term used by Ephemea Archival to describe non-standard military documents and/or books without an official publication prefix.
FM
Field Manual - Official US Army publications designed to provide instructional guidance and procedures for equipment and tactics in the field.
TM
Technical Manual - Detailed US military publications covering technical aspects of weapons, vehicles, operational maintenance, and tactical equipment.
AFM
Air Force Manual - Primary administrative and instructional publications issued by the United States Air Force.
NAVMED
Navy Medicine - Reference publications and training directives managed by the US Navy Bureau of Medicine and Surgery.
NAVPERS
Navy Personnel - Manuals, training courses, and instructional texts issued by the Bureau of Naval Personnel.
OPNAV
Office of the Chief of Naval Operations - Operational directives, regulations, and standardized manuals produced for the US Navy fleet.
EM
Education Manual - Special instructional texts published under the United States Armed Forces Institute (USAFI) during wartime and post-war eras.
NAVWEARSCHFAC
Navy Weather Research Facility - Specialized technical papers and scientific guidelines issued regarding meteorological and weather-routing hazards.
NWC
Naval Weapons Center - Technical papers and research studies published by the US Navy's primary weapons research and development installation (now NAWCWD China Lake).
NAVOCEANO
Naval Oceanographic Office - Hydrographic, geomorphological, and oceanographic research technical reports distributed by the US Navy.
USMA
United States Military Academy - Publications, outlines, and legal text procedures historically utilized or published by West Point.
